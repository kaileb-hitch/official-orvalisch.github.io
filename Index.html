About Ørvalisch

Ørvalisch is a constructed language built on strategic, linguistic, and cultural foundations. Its visual identity centers on a distinctive orthography that blends elements from Germanic and Romance language families, creating a unique aesthetic.

Whether you're here to explore the lexicon, understand the grammar, or simply appreciate the craft behind a constructed language, you'll find a community of speakers committed to preserving and developing this intricate system of communication.

Special Characters & Phonetic Features

ß (Eszett)

This character has context-dependent pronunciation:

  • Standard: /z/ (z sound) — Most common usage, as in "ßacht" (night)
  • With vowels: /bs/ or /bz/ blend — Creates a complex sibilant, as in "ßai" (blue) pronounced "bsī" or "bzī"
  • The ß serves as a unifying element across Germanic linguistic roots
Ø (O with stroke)

Represents the German-influenced "ø" sound, a rounded front vowel similar to French "eu". Examples:

  • Betøl (beetle)
  • Crøbe (crab)
  • Terø (tea)
Þ (Thorn)

An Old English/Icelandic character representing the "th" sound (/θ/ or /ð/). Examples:

  • Þeutsany (Germany)
  • Þru (you)
  • Þu (do)
Diphthongs & Complex Vowels

Ørvalisch employs several diphthongs for richer expression:

  • /aɪ/ (like "I" in English) — as in certain word stress patterns
  • /aʊ/ (like "ou" in English) — appearing in borrowed or specialized terms
  • /ɔɪ/ (like "oy" in English) — creating distinctive tonal qualities
